My GIS Story and Mission

My love of maps goes back to my childhood, when I used to look through my family's road atlas and dream of places to go. I was lucky to have a 3D digital relief map in a classroom, which I would love to run my hands over the Rockies and dream of seeing them in person. My formal GIS journey began after my 11 year old position was eliminated and I began thinking about a new career. I enrolled in the Itasca Community College online GIS Certificate Program and loved it. I went on to intern and work in local government Public Works, where I work today. GIS Cartography is my primary passion; creating well-designed aesthetically-pleasing and informative maps. I also love Spatial Analysis and bring my STEM degree and experience in analytical thinking and problem-solving. I have a keen eye for image interpretation from my GIS work experience so far. I'm also passionate about collaborating with others to achieve excellence.

My Life

My husband Matt and I, and our 4 cats live in St. Louis Park Minnesota, just west of Minneapolis. I enjoy travelling, which goes along with my love of maps. On weekend, you'll find me enjoying the outdoors in my backyard garden, or in a local or state park enjoying outdoor activities like hiking, camping, and kayaking!

Volunteer Experience

I primarily volunteer for a foster-based cat rescue called The Kittens Cradle. I am currently in a leadership role as a Foster Support Coordinator, serving as a communications conduit between our directors and foster volunteers. I provide the fosters with supplies and support for successful adoptions. I also foster cats and kittens and provide administrative support.

After I complete my Capstone GIS Certificate, I plan to volunteer my GIS skills where there is need, especially with racial equity work.
